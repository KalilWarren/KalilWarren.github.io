About Me

I am a PhD student in the Cognitive and Neural Sciences program at the University of South Carolina. My work sits at the intersection of cognitive science, neuroscience, and statistics education.

I am broadly interested in how people learn and reason about statistical and quantitative concepts, and how those processes are reflected in the brain. My research draws on behavioral experiments, neuroimaging methods, and computational modeling to understand human cognition.
